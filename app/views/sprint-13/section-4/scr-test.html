Can your GP share a summary of your care with other healthcare professionals?

{% call fieldset({ legend: { text: "", classes: "nhsuk-fieldset__legend--l", isPageHeading: true } }) %}

Your GP surgery needs permission to create and share a summary of your care. This is called a Summary Care Record (SCR). Your SCR can then be shared nationally with those involved in your care or treatment.

Your SCR has information about the medicines you take, any allergies or adverse reactions you might have and may contain additional information. This additional information has details of any significant illnesses and health problems, operations and vaccinations you have had, how you would like to be treated (such as where you would prefer to receive care), what support you might need and who should be contacted for more information about you. Your SCR can only be shared with healthcare staff treating you and provides them with vital information from your health record.
