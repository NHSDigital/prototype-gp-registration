Depending on your type of service or your relationship to a former member of the armed forces you may need to do one of the following when you leave the Defence Medical Services (DMS):

  • Former regulars
    You should have been given a FMED133A form (sometimes called an FMED1 form) when you left the UK armed forces. You should give this to your GP at the earliest opportunity. Your GP will then arrange for your records to be transferred.

  • Former reservists
    If you had previously received healthcare from the DMS you should have been given an FMED133A form. If you haven't receive one and think you should have you can follow the NHS Step by step guide for service leavers.

  • Family of former members of UK armed forces
    You should inform your GP surgery who will arrange the transfer of you medical records from the DMS.

  • All other former regulars and reservists
    If you have previously been in the UK armed forces, but your care has recently been with an NHS GP, your records will be transferred from your previous GP to your new one.
