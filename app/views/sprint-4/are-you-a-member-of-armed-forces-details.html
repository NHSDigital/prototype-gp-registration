For recent former regular members

You should have been given a FMED133A form (sometimes called an FMED1 form) when you left the Armed Forces. You should give this to your GP at the earliest opportunity. Your GP will then arrange for your records to be transferred.

For former reservists

If you had previously received healthcare from theÂ Defence Medical Services you should have been given an FMED133A form. If you haven't receive one and think you should have you can follow the NHS Step by step guide for service leavers.

For family members of former Armed Forces members

You should inform your GP surgery who will arrange the transfer of you medical records from the Defence Medical Service.

For all other former regular members and reservists

If you have previously been in the Armed Forces, but your care has recently been with an NHS GP, your records will be transferred from your previous GP to your new one.
