How you self-describe

You may choose to self-describe. Your GP surgery will use this information to help them improve the experience of everyone accessing primary care services.

For example, you may self describe as:

  • Bisexual - someone who is attracted to men and women
  • Gay or lesbian - Men who are attracted to men, and women who are attracted to women
  • Heterosexua (straight) - Men who are attracted to women, and women who are attracted to men

Changing your home address will update your NHS record and the address your GP surgery has for you.

Some GP surgeries will only accept patients that live within the area they cover. This is called their catchment area.

If you continue with this change and [NAME OF SURGERY] is not accepting registrations from outside their catchment area:

  • you may not be offered home visits
  • it could affect referrals for hospital tests and treatment, or community health services
