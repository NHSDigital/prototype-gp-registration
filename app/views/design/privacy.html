1. How we use your personal information

This privacy policy explains how we use your personal data when you use the ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service. You can use this online service to register with a GP surgery or change your GP surgery, so that you can access NHS primary care services in England.

This service is available via web browser and the NHS App.

You should read this policy alongside the Terms of use For this service.

1.1 In this privacy policy the following terms have the following meanings:

  • Controller: "The person or organisation (alone or with others) who decides what personal data to process and how it will be used".
  • Processor: "An organisation which processes personal data on behalf of, and under the instruction, of the controller".
  • Special Category Data: "Sensitive personal data given special protection in data protection law including personal data about your health".

2. The ‘NHS GP Registration’ service and who we are

The 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service is provided by NHS England. The service allows you to apply to register with a GP surgery or change your GP surgery.

The Secretary of State for Health and Social Care has issued NHS England legal document called a ‘Direction’ under section 254 of the Health and Social Care Act 2012, which instructs NHS England to provide the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service. You can read this Direction here.

Under data protection law, NHS England is a controller for the personal data you provide when you use the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service.

NHS England is also the controller for the following services which are used to provide the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service:

  • NHS login, which provides patients with a simple, secure and re-usable way to access multiple digital health and care services, including 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery'.
  • The Personal Demographics Service (PDS) which is the national electronic database of NHS patient details such as name, address, date of birth and NHS Number (known as demographic information).
  • The ‘Demographics Notify a Patient’ service, which provides patients with a notification to inform them that their change of GP has been successful. If you are an NHS App user, these notifications may be sent through the NHS App Messaging Service. For further information se NHS App and Account privacy policy

4. How we use your data

4.1 Using Personal Demographic Service (PDS) to retrieve your patient record

While using this service, PDS is used to match the answers to your questions to your PDS record and in some cases to pre-populate some answers if you have used NHS login.

4.2 Using NHS login to pre-populate your personal data

It is possible to use your NHS login account to register with the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service. The benefit is that the information which we hold for your NHS login account will be used to set up your ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service and save you having to provide the same information again. During the registration process, we will ask you for your permission to use the NHS login information for the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' registration. See NHS login's Privacy Notice and Terms and Conditions for more information.

4.3 Securely send your application to your chosen GP surgery

The data collected by this service, is securely sent to your chosen GP surgery so that they can process your application to register with them.

Your chosen GP surgery will become the data controller for your application when it is securely received by them.

4.4 Service improvement, audit and troubleshooting

We look at how the service is being used to help us make it better. To do this we put small files called “analytic cookies” on to your device using a software called These cookies are optional. The information collected includes: the type of device you used, your browser type, your operating system, the date/time you used the service and how you interacted with the service. For more information, please see our cookie policy.

We also store technical log data for audit and troubleshooting (bug/fix) purposes and to make improvements to the service.

The personal data collected by the service will be de-identified and analysed to optimize the service and for policy developments. Information on how many people use the service and its performance will also be used to publish anonymous statistical dashboards. See log/audit data in the table below for more information.

We also count how many users have Javascript disabled or use a browser which does not support javascript in order to improve the accessibility of the service.

4.5 Send you a notification to inform you that your change of GP has been successful

The ‘Demographics Notify a Patient’ service will send you a notification via NHS App, email, SMS or post to inform you that your registration has been successful and that you can now access services at your new GP practice. This will be in cases where your new GP practice has signed up to this notification service.

8. Your rights

Data protection law provides you with a number of rights. You can exercise your rights by contacting NHS England’s Data Protection Officer at enquiries@nhsdigital.nhs.uk.

  • Right to be informed – this privacy policy explains how we use your personal data to provide this service.
  • Right of access – to get a copy of your data submitted to this service, you can request this by completing a Subject Access Request (SAR). If you would like a copy of your GP record, please contact your GP Surgery.
  • Right to rectification – you can use this service to provide up to date contact information which will be sent to the GP Surgery you are registering with. If after registering with this service you need to update your details, you will need to contact your GP practice.
  • Right to restrict processing – you have the right to ask us to limit the way we use your data if you think the data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to withdraw consent – you have the right to withdraw consent for analytical cookies to be placed on your device by selecting ‘Reject analytics cookies’.See our cookie policy.
