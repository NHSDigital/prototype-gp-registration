1. Introduction

1.1 We (NHS England) have developed a digital GP registration service called 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery', accessed via this website through which you can register for or change a General Practitioner to access NHS primary care in England. To find out more about who we are and our role, visit the NHS England website.

Contacting us If you need to get in touch please make contact via your GP.

3. NHS Register with a GP Surgery’

3.1 ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ is free. To use ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ you must be registering with a GP surgery in England.

3.2 If you access or attempt to access ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ from outside England you are responsible for complying with any local laws that apply to you in the country from which you are using 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery'.

3.3 You must access the service from the link provided by 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' for the GP you are registering with.

3.4 You must answer all the mandatory questions to enable GPs to process your registration details.

3.5 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' asks a series of questions, your answers will become your application to register with a GP.

3.6 If you already have an NHS login account (with any level of identity verification) you can use this to access the service. If you use NHS login, some of the answers will be pre-populated for you. You don't need an NHS login account to use this service. Find out more information about NHS login.

3.7 When you submit your application, all your answers are shared with the GP.

3.8 The GP controls and owns the registration process, it is not automated on the submission of your application. Any queries about your application should be directed to the GP. The GP will respond to you directly to confirm the outcome of your application.

‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ is not a medical device and so is not regulated by the Medicines and Health Regulatory Agency (MHRA).
