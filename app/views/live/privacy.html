1. How we use your personal information

This privacy policy explains how we use your personal data when you use the ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service. You can use this online service to register with a GP surgery or change your GP surgery, so that you can access NHS primary care services in England.

This service is available via web browser and the NHS App.

You should read this policy alongside the Terms of use For this service.

1.1 In this privacy policy the following terms have the following meanings:

  • Controller: "The person or organisation (alone or with others) who decides what personal data to process and how it will be used"
  • Processor: "An organisation which processes personal data on behalf of, and under the instruction, of the controller"
  • Special Category Data: "Sensitive personal data given special protection in data protection law including personal data about your health"

2. The ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service and who we are

The 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service is provided by NHS England. The service allows you to apply to register with a GP surgery or change your GP surgery.

The Secretary of State for Health and Social Care has issued NHS England a legal document called a ‘Direction’ under section 254 of the Health and Social Care Act 2012, which instructs NHS England to provide the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service. You can read this Direction here.

Under data protection law, NHS England is a controller for the personal data you provide when you use the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service.

NHS England is also the controller for the following services which are used to provide the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service:

  • NHS login, which provides patients with a simple, secure and re-usable way to access multiple digital health and care services, including 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery'.
  • The Personal Demographics Service (PDS) which is the national electronic database of NHS patient details such as name, address, date of birth and NHS Number (known as demographic information).
  • The ‘Demographics Notify a Patient’ service, which provides patients with a notification to inform them that their change of GP has been successful. If you are an NHS App user, these notifications may be sent through the NHS App Messaging Service. For further information see NHS App privacy policy: messaging services.

Information captured against your NHS Register with a GP Surgery session ID about your use of the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery', such as the time of use, actions you took using the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery', and associated technical log events.

NHS Register with a GP Surgery demographic data

The personal data you provide to register with a GP surgery such as your name, address, date of birth, NHS number, your email address, contact telephone numbers, your sex and ethnicity.
NHS Register with a GP Surgery Medical & Lifestyle Questionnaire data (optional)

The personal data you provide to inform your new GP of your medical history and lifestyle, such as your existing or pre-existing medical conditions, allergies and current medications.
European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) data

If you are a visitor from outside of the UK, we collect EHIC details to determine your charging eligibility for NHS care. Providing this information is optional. If you choose to provide this, we ask for your:

  • Personal Identification number
  • Identification number of your EHIC card
  • Identification number of the institution
  • Expiry date
NHS Register with a GP Surgery application metadata

The personal data that is derived by the service from the demographic information you provide and is auto generated. Metadata includes: registration type, date and time of submission, application reference number, NHS login identity proofing level, PDS match, PDS date of death, PDS matched NHS number, the organisation code for your current GP practice, PDS number (if different to patient entered NHS number), confirmation of EHIC details sent to National Back Office.

4. How we use your data

4.1 Using Personal Demographic Service (PDS) to retrieve and update your PDS record

We use the demographic data you provide to match you to your PDS record. This helps us to find your NHS number so that we can send this to your chosen GP surgery along with your application. If you provide new contact information such as a new phone number or email address, we use this to update your PDS record. It is important for the PDS to hold up to date contact information, so that the NHS can contact you to provide you with care e.g. send you appointment reminders and invites to health screening services.

4.2 Using NHS login to pre-populate your personal data

It is possible to use your NHS login account to register with the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service. The benefit is that the information which we hold for your NHS login account will be used to set up your ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service and save you having to provide the same information again. During the registration process, we will ask you for your permission to use the NHS login information for the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' registration. See NHS login's Privacy Notice and Terms and Conditions for more information.

4.3 Securely send your application to your chosen GP surgery

The data collected by this service, is securely sent to your chosen GP surgery so that they can process your application to register with them.

Your chosen GP surgery will become the data controller for your application when it is securely received by them.

After you have submitted your registration application at the end of the Register with a GP Surgery service, your chosen GP surgery may want to ask you some additional questions. If you click the link to answer these additional questions, you will be taken to your chosen GP surgery’s website. The only information we share to the GP surgery’s website is a unique reference number associated with your application. This will allow your chosen GP surgery to link your registration application to any additional questions you answer.

4.4 Service improvement, audit and troubleshooting

We look at how the service is being used to help us make it better. To do this we put small files called “analytic cookies” on to your device using a software called These cookies are optional. The information collected includes: the type of device you used, your browser type, your operating system, the date/time you used the service and how you interacted with the service. For more information, please see our cookie policy.

We also store technical log data for audit and troubleshooting (bug/fix) purposes and to make improvements to the service.

The personal data collected by the service will be de-identified and analysed to optimize the service and for policy developments. Information on how many people use the service and its performance will also be used to publish anonymous statistical dashboards. See log/audit data in the table below for more information.

We also count how many users have Javascript disabled or use a browser which does not support javascript in order to improve the accessibility of the service.

4.5 Send you notifications about your GP application

If you provide an email address when using Register with a GP surgery online, the service will send you an email notification to confirm that your application has been submitted to your chosen GP surgery.

The service may also send you a notification via the NHS App, email, SMS and post to inform you if your registration is accepted by your chosen GP. This will only be in cases when your new GP practice has enabled this feature in the service.

Your chosen GP may also send you a notification to let you know if your application is accepted or rejected.

5. Our legal basis and how long NHS hold your data

Our legal basis to process your personal data to provide this service is:

  • Legal obligation - Article 6(1)(c) of UK GDPR. This is because the Secretary of State for Health and Social Care has issued us with a Direction to provide this service. This Direction is called the Register with a GP Surgery Directions 2022.
  • Consent – Article 6(1)(a) of UK GDPR, if you choose to accept optional analytics cookies. See our cookie policy for more information.

We also need an additional legal basis in the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018) to use data which is extra sensitive. This is known as ‘special categories of personal data’. Our legal basis to use data relating to your health is:

  • Health or social care – Article 9(2)(h) of UK GDPR, plus Schedule 1, Part 1, Paragraph 2 “Health or social care purposes” of DPA 2018

9. Your rights

Data protection law provides you with a number of rights. You can exercise your rights by contacting NHS England’s Data Protection Officer at england.dpo@nhs.net.

  • Right to be informed – this privacy policy explains how we use your personal data to provide this service.
  • Right of access – to get a copy of your data submitted to this service, you can request this by completing a Subject Access Request (SAR). If you would like a copy of your GP record, please contact your GP Surgery.
  • Right to rectification – you can use this service to provide up to date contact information which will be sent to the GP Surgery you are registering with. If after registering with this service you need to update your details, you will need to contact your GP practice.
  • Right to restrict processing – you have the right to ask us to limit the way we use your data if you think the data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to withdraw consent – you have the right to withdraw consent for analytical cookies to be placed on your device by selecting ‘Reject analytics cookies’.See our cookie policy.
