Changing your home address will update your NHS record.

It's important your address is up to date so that you continue to get:

  • letters or test results
  • home visits and urgent care, including out of hours care
  • community services, such as physiotherapy and midwife appointments

If your new address is outside of your practice catchment area

Your current GP surgery may contact you to confirm you've moved outside of their catchment area. You might:

  • stay with your current surgery, but with some changes to the care you get
  • have to find a new GP surgery

This is because your current GP might not be the right place for you to get care if your address has changed.

If your current surgery doesn't accept patients outside of their catchment area, you will have 30 days to find a new GP to register with.

Registering with a GP surgery takes about 15 minutes and automatically updates your NHS record.

Some of your health information may not show in the NHS App while your records are sent to your new GP surgery.

When to speak to your current GP first

Speak to your current GP if you:

  • are on a waiting list for elective surgery
  • have any GP appointments you are waiting for
  • take any prescription medications
