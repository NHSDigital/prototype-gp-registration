1. How we use your personal information

This privacy policy explains how we use your personal data when you use the ‘Self-Enrolment Service’. This service allows you to onboard your practice to the ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service, so that you can offer your patients the ability to register with your GP surgery online.

You should read this policy alongside the Terms of use for this service.

1.1 In this privacy policy the following terms have the following meanings:

  • Controller: "The person or organisation (alone or with others) who decides what personal data to process and how it will be used".

2. The 'GP Registration Self-Enrolment' service and who we are

The 'GP Registration Self Enrollment' service allows you to onboard your practice to the ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service, so that you can offer your patients the ability to register at your surgery online.

The organisations involved in providing the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service and their roles are:

2.1. NHS England

NHS England leads the National Health Service (NHS) in England. It sets the priorities and direction of the NHS. A lot of the work NHS England does involves the commissioning of healthcare services in England.

Find out more on the NHS England webiste

NHS England has a legal duty to provide GP registration services, under Schedule 3, Part 2, paragraph 17 of the National Health Service (General Medical Services Contracts) Regulations 2015.

HS England has issued NHS Digital with a legal document called a ‘Direction’ under section 254 of the Health and Social Care Act 2012, which instructs NHS Digital to provide the 'NHS Register with a GP Surgery' service. You can read this Direction here.

2.2. NHS Digital

NHS Digital was set up by the Department of Health and Social Care in April 2013 and is an executive non-departmental public body that provides national information, data and IT systems for health and care services.

Find out more on the NHS Digital website

NHS Digital is a joint data controller with NHS England to provide the NHS Register with a GP Surgery service.

3. What information we collect about you

Category of information Data items
Practice Mailbox (which may include contact information)

In order for you to register your GP Practice to offer the ‘NHS Register with a GP Surgery’ service to patients, we need to collect a contact email address (@nhs.net) from your surgery. This is usually the shared mailbox for the practice.

Practice information

This includes:

  • ODS code
  • Address
  • Contact email address
  • hone Number

This information is collected from Service Search.

4. How we use your data

Practice Mailbox

We use this email address to:

  • Send a verification code, to verify this email is correct
  • Send you confirmation of your registration
  • Send patient registrations, which includes confidential and sensitive patient data

Practice Information

We use this to configure your registration service and contact you in the event of any problems or if we need to collect feedback. We also ask you some questions about your practice, which are used to configure which questions your patients will see.

6. Where your data is stored

We only store and process your personal data within the UK.
